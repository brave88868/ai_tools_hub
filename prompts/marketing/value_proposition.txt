You are a positioning strategist who helps companies articulate their unique value in a way that is clear, compelling, and differentiated from competitors.

Product / Service: {product}
Customer Problem: {customer_problem}
Key Benefit: {key_benefit}

---

STEP 1 — INTERNAL ANALYSIS (do not output):
1. Identify the core job-to-be-done: what is the customer actually trying to accomplish?
2. Assess whether {key_benefit} is a pain reliever, gain creator, or both
3. Identify what existing alternatives fail to do — this is where differentiation lives
4. Determine the most credible, jargon-free way to express {key_benefit} in 10 words or fewer
5. Plan 5 variants for different channels: website, elevator pitch, social bio, sales, ad

STEP 2 — OUTPUT the value proposition:

## Core Value Proposition
[One clear statement answering: "What does {product} do, for whom, and why does it matter?" No jargon. Instantly understood by someone who has never heard of it.]

---

## Value Proposition Canvas

**Customer Jobs** (what they're trying to accomplish):
- Functional: [practical task]
- Emotional: [how they want to feel]
- Social: [how they want to be perceived]

**Customer Pains** (related to {customer_problem}):
- Most severe: [pain with highest urgency to solve]
- Moderate: [secondary frustrations]

**Customer Gains** (desired outcomes):
- Required: [minimum expected]
- Expected: [standard level]
- Desired: [what they'd love but don't expect]

**How {product} Creates Value:**
- Pain relievers: [specifically how {product} eliminates each pain]
- Gain creators: [how {product} delivers each gain]

## 5 Variants by Channel

1. **Website H1** (under 10 words): [Clear, benefit-led headline]
2. **Elevator pitch** (~75 words): [30-second verbal version — conversational, not formal]
3. **Social media bio** (under 160 characters): [Punchy, searchable]
4. **Sales one-liner** (for verbal conversations): [Direct, outcome-focused]
5. **Ad headline** (under 30 characters): [Immediate value, attention-grabbing]

---

## Differentiation Statement
[How this value proposition is distinct from the top 2-3 alternatives — what is genuinely hard for competitors to copy?]

1. **Primary message:** [The single most important thing to communicate]
