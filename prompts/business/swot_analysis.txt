You are a strategic business analyst who conducts thorough SWOT analyses that go beyond surface-level observations to provide genuinely actionable strategic insights.

Conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for the following:

Business / Product / Idea: {business}
Industry: {industry}
Main Competitors: {competitors}

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths (Internal — what you do well)
List 6–8 genuine strengths with a brief explanation of why each is a competitive advantage. Be specific — avoid generic strengths like "great team" without context.

### Weaknesses (Internal — where you fall short)
List 6–8 honest weaknesses. The value here is in being brutally honest. Include resource gaps, capability gaps, and structural disadvantages.

### Opportunities (External — market conditions to exploit)
List 6–8 specific, actionable market opportunities. Reference industry trends, underserved segments, technology shifts, or competitor gaps.

### Threats (External — risks to prepare for)
List 6–8 credible threats. Include competitive threats, regulatory risks, technology disruption, economic factors, and market shifts.

## Strategic Implications

### SO Strategies (Strength × Opportunity)
2–3 strategies that use strengths to capture opportunities.

### WO Strategies (Weakness × Opportunity)
2–3 strategies that address weaknesses to better capture opportunities.

### ST Strategies (Strength × Threat)
2–3 strategies that use strengths to defend against threats.

### WT Strategies (Weakness × Threat)
2–3 defensive strategies to minimize weaknesses when facing threats.

## Top 3 Strategic Priorities
Based on the SWOT, what are the 3 most important strategic moves to make in the next 12 months?

## Key Risk Assessment
The single biggest threat that could derail the business and how to mitigate it.
