You are a startup pitch coach and investor relations expert who has helped founders raise millions from angel investors, VCs, and institutional funds.

Create an investor-ready pitch deck outline for the following:

Company Name: {company_name}
Problem You Solve: {problem}
Your Solution: {solution}
Traction / Metrics: {traction}

Generate a complete slide-by-slide outline:

## Slide 1: Cover
- Company name, tagline, and presenter info
- Design note: simple, brand-aligned, memorable

## Slide 2: The Problem
- Vivid description of the problem (use data + story)
- Who experiences this problem and how frequently
- Why existing solutions fail
- The cost of the problem (financial or emotional)

## Slide 3: The Solution
- What {company_name} does in one clear sentence
- How it works (3-step process if possible)
- Key insight or "secret" that makes this work
- Demo or product screenshot placeholder

## Slide 4: Market Opportunity
- TAM / SAM / SOM with sources
- Market timing: why now?
- Macro trends supporting the opportunity

## Slide 5: Business Model
- How you make money (revenue streams)
- Unit economics (LTV, CAC, payback period if available)
- Pricing model summary

## Slide 6: Traction
- Key metrics: {traction}
- Growth chart placeholder
- Customer logos, testimonials, or case study highlights

## Slide 7: Competitive Landscape
- Competitor matrix (3–5 competitors vs. key dimensions)
- Your defensible position and moat

## Slide 8: Go-to-Market Strategy
- Primary acquisition channel
- Launch strategy and scale plan

## Slide 9: Team
- Founder bios (relevant experience + why this team wins)
- Key hires needed

## Slide 10: The Ask
- Amount raising and instrument (SAFE, equity, convertible note)
- Use of funds (breakdown by %)
- Milestones this round achieves
- Expected runway

## Slide 11: Vision
- Where {company_name} is in 5 years
- Exit possibilities (optional)

---
## Investor Narrative Thread
The connecting story that runs through all 11 slides — the "why this, why now, why us" arc in 3–4 sentences.

## Common Investor Questions
5 tough questions investors will ask and suggested talking points.
